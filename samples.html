KMP

pattern matching educational software

An educational software on the Knuth Morris Pratt (KMP) algorithm that simulated and explained it step by step on carefully selected examples, as well as evaluated the understanding of the algorithm.

See next project →

Pattern

An instance of the problem is presented as a DNA substring matching case, where the student should find a pattern in the genome.

Matching

The software showcases the source code, as well as the values of the variables involved in the computation. Here the software analyzes one of the strings - a necessary pre-processing step in the KMP algorithm.

Credits

Winner of a national contest, the software was implemented by Vlad Manea and Vlad Tudose, and coordinated by professors Emanuela Cerchez and Marinel Șerban. An improved version was acquired by the company Siveco Romania.

Habitlab

iOS application for habit formation

An iOS app to help people build good habits. Featured a menu with scheduling, programs, progress tracking and social activities, for running, reading, unplugging and reconnecting with friends.

See next project →

Schedule

Habitlab helped its users discover a smart, sticky and social way to building good habits. A habit consisted of: scheduling a time, inspiring friends, overviewing progress, doing a simple activity and advancing levels.

Advance

Each habit was organized into programs tailored for increasing levels of difficulty, levels with increasing time spans in weeks, and loops with one single, well-defined action. The user was notified before each loop.

Achieve

Habitlab implemented 4 types of habits: unplugging, reading, running and reconnecting. Loops of all types of habits shared the same habit formation structure: trigger, action and reward.

Share

For some habit types, the user could send thoughts to a close group of friends. In the image, the user is halfway with the unplug program and may feel energized, happy or just smart.

Follow

The user could stay up to date with the progress of the closest friends in a social feed with rich content through a follow > approve model. Sometimes inspired, sometimes inspiring, the users met in a social feed with rich content, love and commenting options.

Credits

Qualified to the Thinkubator Copenhagen think-tank and incubator in 2016, Habitlab was designed by Christina Lauer and implemented by Vlad Manea.

.campion

educational archive

A web application that organized the 1500 algorithmic tasks in 10 years of the .campion performance learning program, made easy to find and linked to original articles and educational software lessons.

See next project →

Prepare

All content is public, but one needs an account to submit problem task solutions. The page with problem tasks contains information about the difficulty of each problem, based on the success rate of the past submitted solutions.

Compete

The submitted sources allow the archive to provide the top ranking students, along with their scores and success rates. One person on this list obtained a medal at the International Olympiad in Informatics!

Search

The archive allows full search of the problem tasks, articles, software lessons, competitors, professors, and more. The search can range from broad to granular, targeting specific subsets of attributes.

Credits

Awarded at a national conference, the archive was implemented by Vlad Manea and professors Emanuela Cerchez and Marinel Șerban. Vlad Tudose and Vlad Manea were members of the .campion program.

DP

dynamic programming educational software

Explained three dynamic programming problems step by step: the discrete knapsack, the longest increasing subsequence and the triangle sum.

See next project →

Triangles

The first problem asks to find the maximum sum by moving down a triangle of numbers. Here one can see the decision: go bottom left or bottom right?

Sequences

The second dynamic problem asks how to hop from left to right so that the hops sum up to as much as possible. Here a solution to the problem is constructed.

Knapsack

The third problem is the discrete knapsack on a travel example: one must place a limited total weight of various items while maximizing their total value.

Credits

The software was developed by Vlad Manea and Vlad Tudose, coordinated by professors Emanuela Cerchez and Marinel Șerban, when the Vlads were interested specifically in algorithms.
