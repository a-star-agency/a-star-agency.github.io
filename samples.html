KMP

pattern matching educational software

An educational software on the Knuth Morris Pratt (KMP) algorithm that simulated and explained it step by step on carefully selected examples, as well as evaluated the understanding of the algorithm.

See the next ↓

Pattern

The software presents an instance of the problem as a DNA substring matching case, where the student should find a pattern in the genome.

Matching

The software showcases the source code, as well as the values of the variables involved in the computation. Here the software analyzes one of the strings — a necessary pre-processing step in the KMP algorithm.

Credits

Winner of the Siveco Cup national contest, the software was implemented by Vlad Manea, Vlad Tudose, and Andrei Albeșteanu, coordinated by professors Emanuela Cerchez and Marinel Șerban. The company Siveco Romania acquired an improved version of this software.

Habitlab

iOS application for habit formation

An iOS app to help people build good habits. Featured a menu with scheduling, programs, progress tracking and social activities, for running, reading, unplugging and reconnecting with friends.

See the next ↓

Schedule

Habitlab helped its users discover a smart, sticky and social way to building good habits. A habit consisted of: scheduling a time, inspiring friends, overviewing progress, doing a simple activity and advancing levels.

Advance

We split each habit into programs tailored for increasing levels of difficulty, levels with growing time spans in weeks, and loops with one single, well-defined action.

Achieve

Habitlab implemented four types of habits: unplugging, reading, running and reconnecting. Loops of all kinds of practices shared the same habit formation structure: trigger, action, and reward.

Share

For some habit types, the user could send thoughts to a close group of friends. In the image, the user is halfway with the unplug program and may feel energized, happy or just smart.

Follow

The user could stay up to date with the progress of the closest friends in a social feed with rich content through a follow > approve model. Sometimes inspired, sometimes inspiring, the users met in a social feed with rich content, love and commenting options.

Credits

Qualified to the Thinkubator Copenhagen think-tank and incubator, the Habitlab app was designed by Christina Lauer, and implemented by Vlad Manea and others.

.campion

educational archive

A web application that organized the 1500 algorithmic tasks in 10 years of the .campion performance learning program made easy to find. It links to original articles and educational software lessons.

See the next ↓

Prepare

All content is public, but one needs an account to submit problem task solutions. The page with problem tasks contains information about the difficulty of each problem, based on the success rate of the past provided source code.

Compete

The submitted sources allow the archive to provide the top ranking students, along with their scores and success rates. One person on this list obtained a medal at the International Olympiad in Informatics!

Search

The archive allows the full search of the problem tasks, articles, software lessons, competitors, professors, and more. The search can range from broad to granular, targeting specific subsets of attributes.

Credits

The software was implemented by Vlad Manea coordinated by professors Emanuela Cerchez and Marinel Șerban, and awarded at the national virtual learning conference.

DP

dynamic programming educational software

Explained three dynamic programming problems step by step: the discrete knapsack, the maximal increasing subsequence, and the triangle sum.

See the next ↓

Triangles

The first problem asks to find the maximum sum by moving down a triangle of numbers. Here one can see the decision: go bottom left or bottom right?

Sequences

The second dynamic problem asks how to hop from left to right so that the hops sum up to as much as possible. Here a solution to the problem is constructed.

Knapsack

The third problem is the discrete knapsack on a travel example: one must place a limited total weight of various items while maximizing their cumulative value.

Credits

Vlad Manea, Vlad Tudose, and Ioana Marțin implemented the software, coordinated by professors Emanuela Cerchez and Marinel Șerban.
